      FAQ

      How do US universities convert Indian percentage to GPA?

      There is no universal formula. Some universities evaluate transcripts internally, while others rely on credential evaluation agencies.

      Is 70% in India equal to 3.5 GPA in the US?

      Not exactly. Interpretation depends on grading distribution and institution.

      Does WES use the same GPA conversion?

      No. Credential evaluators may use proprietary institutional formulas.

      Is CGPA treated differently from percentage?

      Yes. Always use the primary metric on your official transcript.

      What GPA is competitive for top US programs?

      Equivalent of 3.6+ is typically competitive, but research, projects, and recommendations matter heavily.

      Should I enter CGPA or percentage if I have both?

      Use the primary number your official transcript emphasizes. If both appear, pick the one most consistently used across semesters.

      Does a 3-year Indian degree affect US admissions?

      Some US programs accept 3-year degrees, some prefer 4-year. Always check the specific program’s eligibility rules.

      Do backlogs or repeats matter as much as GPA?

      They can matter, but context matters. Strong improvement, solid projects, and strong recommendations can reduce the impact.

      Why can WES/ECE conversions differ from this tool?

      Credential evaluators may use institution-specific rules, grade distributions, and proprietary methods. This tool is an estimate for planning.

      Does a 4.0 here mean I’m guaranteed admission?

      No. A high GPA helps, but admissions depends on your full profile: projects, research, experience, SOP, and fit with the program.

      What’s a “good” GPA for MSCS / Data Science / AI in the US?

      For highly selective programs, 3.6+ is often competitive, but strong projects and research can be just as important depending on the program.

      How important are projects compared to GPA for CS?

      Projects can be a major differentiator. A strong, deployed project with clear impact can significantly strengthen an application.

      Should I include MOOCs and certifications?

      They help most when they support a real project. Use them to fill gaps, then show proof through work (GitHub, reports, deployments).

      Do US universities still require the GRE?

      Many programs are test-optional. Some still recommend it for certain applicants. Check each program page directly.

      Do IELTS/TOEFL requirements depend on my university language?

      Often yes, but policies vary by school. Some waive English tests if your prior degree was taught in English and documented officially.

      What if my GPA estimate is low. Should I still apply?

      Yes, but apply strategically: broaden your list, strengthen projects, write a strong SOP, and use recommendations that vouch for your ability.

      How should I use the readiness score on this page?

      Treat it like a checklist signal. Use the “Quick fixes” and “Big moves” to decide what to improve next before submitting applications.

      Why does this tool use “smooth bands” instead of one formula?

      Because real evaluations don’t have one universal rule. Smooth bands avoid unrealistic jumps at cutoffs and give a more stable estimate.

      Is this tool only for Computer Science?

      No. The GPA estimate is general. The readiness checklist adapts based on the field you choose.

      Is the readiness score an “admission probability”?

      No. It’s a practical signal to guide improvement, not a prediction or guarantee.

      Important notes

      • Estimates only: no universal rule exists.
      • Context matters: grading culture changes interpretation.
      • No guarantees: this does not predict outcomes.
